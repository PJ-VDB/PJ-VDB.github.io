My Timeline

This timeline shows mostly my educational formation.

  • 2016 - 2017

    Master in Entrepreuneurship and Innovation Management at KTH, Stockholm - Sweden

    I felt that I was missing some essential management competences and was interested in learning more about management and finance, so decided to pursue studies abroad. Being very interested by the energetic transition and renewable energy, I had the opportunity to make my master thesis at Hexicon AB, a swedish startup developing a floating offshore wind turbine concept. I investigated the potential of offshore floating wind turbines in California.

  • 2015 - 2016

    Master in Mechanical Engineering at KU Leuven, Leuven - Belgium

    I was awarded the master thesis prize by Siemens Industry Software for my thesis “Anomaly detection methods in rotating machinery” under supervision of Prof. Ir. Konstantinos Gryllias. The goal was to design a machine learning algorithm that could detect defects in rolling elemnt bearings at very early stages for the use in preventive maintenance applications.

  • 2014 - 2015

    1st Master year in Mechanical Engineering at Chalmers, Göteborg - Sweden

    I studied my first master year abroad in Göteborg, Sweden, as part of the Erasmus program.

  • 2011 - 2014

    Bachelor in Electrical and Mechanical Engineering

    I started of with a bachelor's degree in Mechanical and Electrical Engineering to get a wide but thorough technical knowledge base. Some of my relevant courses: Mechanics, dynamics, kinematics, thermodynamics, signal processing, electrical energy and drives, semiconductor devices, heat transfer, fluid mechanics, numerical mathematics, energy conversion systems, electrical circuits, …

Thesis Award: Anomaly detection methods in rotating machinery

For my studies in Mechanical Engineering, I made my thesis on the topic of anomaly detection methods in rotating machinery. This means that I applied machine learning and pattern recognition methods to automatically detect defects in bearings at early stages. My method was tested on a dataset provided by NASA for which I was later awarded the master thesis prize by Siemens Industry Software.

Drone Dynamics at Chalmers

I spent one year at Chalmers University of Technology as part of my Erasmus. For one of my courses we had to come up with, and program (in C), the dynamic control system to fly a drone (CrazyFlie 2.0). The dynamics of the drone were then also verified in Simulink.

Concept development for Procter & Gamble

In my third bachelor's year at the KU Leuven I had a 1-year engineering project course in co-operation with the R&D department of Procter & Gamble. In a 4-man team, we conceptualized and designed an electromechanical system for one of their new products that had to be added to an existing assembly line. A 3D design of the solution was made in SolidEdge and a software sorting algorithm was written. (*Picture of the real concept could not be shown due to NDA restrictions)

Development of robotic arm

For the project course "Problem Solving" we were given the task to design, construct, and test a robotic arm that could pick up a soda can and serve it without spilling. In our design the fingers were driven seperately by several servo motors, this allowed for very refined movements.
